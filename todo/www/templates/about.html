Rolls-Royce in Indiana

Our facilities in Indianapolis are home to the largest Rolls-Royce manufacturing location in North America. As one of the largest employers in Indianapolis, we employ approximately 4,000 people in central Indiana.


Made in Indiana
Our employees in Indianapolis design, develop, and manufacture key products in the Rolls-Royce portfolio, including the AE 3007 for Embraer’s regional jet, the Citation X and existing defense programs such as Global Hawk. The AE 2100 powers the Lockheed Martin C-130J and the C-27J. The AE 1107 powers the Bell Boeing V-22 Osprey tiltrotor aircraft and the M250 engine powers a variety of helicopters including the Bell 206 and 407. Indianapolis is also key to Rolls-Royce’s participation in the F-35B program. Our components for the F-35B, including the Rolls-Royce LiftSystem®, are assembled near Indianapolis. The Rolls-Royce LiftSystem®, enables short take off and vertical landing (STOVL) operations for the supersonic capable F-35B.


Award Winning Engineering
Our employees from Indianapolis have made major contributions to several projects that have won the prestigious Robert J. Collier Trophy over the course of the last 15 years, highlighting the advanced nature of the work undertaken at the site. The Collier Trophy is presented annually “For the greatest achievement in aeronautics in America.” The award has been called the greatest and most prized of all aeronautical honors in the United States.


Indiana Origins
Indianapolis was formerly the headquarters of the Allison Engine Company, a descendant of the company formed by James Allison on September 14, 1915. James Allison also founded the Indianapolis Motor Speedway. Rolls-Royce purchased Allison in 1995, but the association between the two companies stretches back to the 1920’s when Allison licensed Rolls-Royce to make bearings.

The Company Today
Today, Rolls-Royce is a trusted leader for land, sea and air power solutions worldwide. Comprised of two divisions, Aerospace and Land & Sea, Rolls-Royce is one of the leading engineering companies in the world. More Rolls-Royce engines are built in Indianapolis than anywhere else in the world.


Rolls-Royce is making major investments in the United States because of the contributions the country can make to the global marketplace. Rolls-Royce will double its production capability over the next 10 years in order to meet its global order of $96 billion (£60.1 billion). The investments Rolls-Royce is making in the United States will ensure we continue to deliver excellence to our customers.


Rolls-Royce is the world’s second largest engine manufacturer, and a leader in commercial jet engines. We have a broad customer base comprising more than 500 airlines, 4,000 corporate and utility aircraft and helicopter operators, 160 armed forces, more than 4,000 marine customers, including 70 navies. Rolls-Royce has a total of over 54,000 gas turbine engines in service worldwide.


Renowned Products
The Trent family of Rolls-Royce engines is the most popular commercial fleet model, and used by 50 percent of both Airbus and Boeing’s new generation of widebody aircraft. The AE 3007 and BR700 series engines are strongly positioned in regional and corporate jet sectors.


In the Defense sector, Rolls-Royce continues to be a market leader. Since the C-130’s inception, the Indianapolis-made T56 and AE 2100 engines have powered the aircraft’s numerous variants.


The Marine division is a global leader in marine propulsion, engineering and hydrodynamic expertise. Our product portfolio ranges from vessel design and gas turbine engines to water jet and deck handling equipment. We supply power, propulsion and motion control systems for high-value vessels.

           

The Indianapolis-produced MT7 marine gas turbine, a marine variant of the AE 1107C, has been selected to power the US Navy’s Ship-to-Shore Connector (SSC) hovercraft built by Textron Marine & Land Systems. In addition, Indianapolis supplies RR4500 generator sets to power the US Navy’s new DDG-1000 multi-mission destroyers and has been designed to meet all US Navy requirements.


The offshore marine division has specialized in marine propulsion since the 1970’s. It has supplied over 450 UT-700 series service and support vessels to customers around the world. Rolls-Royce also has a strong presence in cruise and passenger ships, as well as cargo, tugboats and coastal ferries.


University Partnerships
We have an established global network of Research and Technology Centers. Each center addresses a key technology; collectively they tackle a wide range of engineering disciplines. Rolls-Royce maintains an ongoing relationship with Purdue University, one of the most prestigious engineering schools in the country. Over 750 Purdue University engineering graduates work for Rolls-Royce.
