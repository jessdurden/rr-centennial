Rolls-Royce in Indiana

Rolls-Royce facilities in Indianapolis are home to the largest Rolls-Royce manufacturing location in North America. One of the largest employers in Indianapolis, the company employs approximately 4,000 people in central Indiana.


Made in Indiana
Employees in Indianapolis design, develop and manufacture key products in the Rolls-Royce portfolio, including the AE 3007 for Embraer’s regional jet, the Citation X and existing defense programs such as Global Hawk. The AE 2100 powers the Lockheed Martin C-130J and the C-27J. The AE 1107 powers the Bell Boeing V-22 Osprey tiltrotor and the Model 250 engine powers a variety of helicopters including Bell 206 and 407 helicopters. Indianapolis is also key to Rolls-Royce participation in Joint Strike Fighter (JSF). Components for the propulsion system for the F-35B Joint Strike Fighter program are assembled in Indianapolis. The Rolls-Royce LiftSystem® enables short take off and vertical landing (STOVL) operations for the supersonic capable Joint Strike Fighter.


Award Winning Engineering
Personnel from Indianapolis have made major contributions to six projects that have won the prestigious Robert J. Collier Trophy over the course of the last 15 years, highlighting the advanced nature of the work undertaken at the site. The Collier Trophy is presented annually, “For the greatest achievement in aeronautics in America." The award has been called the greatest and most prized of all aeronautical honors in the United States of America.


Indiana Origins
Indianapolis was formerly the headquarters of the Allison Engine Company, a descendant of the company formed by Jim Allison on September 14, 1915, who also founded the Indianapolis Motor Speedway. Rolls-Royce purchased Allison in 1995, but the association between the two companies stretches back to the 1920s when Allison licensed Rolls-Royce to make bearings.

The Company Today
Today, Rolls-Royce is a trusted leader for land, sea and air power solutions worldwide. Comprised of Defense and Civil aerospace, and a Land & Sea Division, Rolls-Royce is one of the foremost engineering companies in the world. More Rolls-Royce engines are built in Indianapolis than anywhere else in the world.


Rolls-Royce is making major investments in the United States because America can compete in the global marketplace. Rolls-Royce will double its production capability over the next 10 years in order to meet its global order book of $96 billion (£60.1 billion) for civil aerospace, defense aerospace, marine and energy products. The investments the company is making in America will ensure we continue to deliver excellence for our customers.


Rolls-Royce is the world's second largest engine manufacturer, and a leader in commercial jet engines.
Rolls-Royce has a broad customer base comprising more than 500 airlines, 4,000 corporate and utility aircraft and helicopter operators, 160 armed forces, more than 4,000 marine customers, including 70 navies, and energy customers in more than 80 countries.. The company has a total of over 54,000 gas turbines in service worldwide.


Renowned Products
The Trent family of Rolls-Royce engines is the most popular commercial fleet model, being used by 50 percent of Airbus and Boeing’s new generation of widebody aircraft. Likewise, the AE 3007 and BR700 series engines are strongly positioned in regional and corporate jet sectors.


In the military transportation sector, Rolls-Royce continues as the undisputed market leader. Since the C-130’s inception, the Indianapolis- made Rolls-Royce T56 and AE 2100 engines have powered the plane’s numerous incarnations.


The company’s marine division is a global leader in marine propulsion, engineering and hydrodynamic expertise; its product portfolio ranges from vessel design and gas turbine engines to water jet and deck handling equipment. The Marine Division’s aim is to supply power, propulsion and motion control systems for high-value vessels.


The Indianapolis-produced MT7 marine gas turbine – a marine variant of the AE 1107C has been selected to power the US Navy’s new Ship-to-Shore Connector (SSC) hovercraft built by Textron Marine & Land Systems. In addition, Indianapolis supplies RR4500 generator sets that power the US Navy’s new DDG-1000 (USS Zumwalt) multi-mission destroyers and has been designed to meet all U.S. Navy requirements.


As for the commercial side of the Marine Division specializes in the offshore sector; since the 1970s, it has supplied over 450 UT-700 series service and support vessels to customers around the world. Rolls-Royce also has a strong presence in cruise and passenger, cargo, tugs and coastal ferries.


University Partnerships
Rolls-Royce in Indianapolis maintains an ongoing relationship with Purdue University, one of the most prestigious engineering schools in the country. Over 750 Purdue engineering graduates work for Rolls-Royce to enhance one of the most distinguishable brands in the world. Many of our 2,000 office employees work out of a 3 building office complex, the Rolls-Royce Meridian Center, located in downtown Indianapolis.


Striving to match its excellent engineering, Rolls-Royce has made several technological investments. Similarly, through high levels of research and development, the company is dedicated to bringing a new level of service and products to market that will facilitate the trickle down of innovation from the military sphere to the civil sphere. Fittingly, the company is uniquely positioned to continue to produce the type of high-quality products expected from a brand name that is synonymous with excellence.
